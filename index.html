Call for Contributions

Submit Your Work

We accept five formats. Choose the one that fits your argument, follow the word counts below, and reference in Harvard (author–date) style.

Book Review

1,000 – 1,500 words

A critical engagement with a recent book on European law, policy or institutions.

Article

2,500 – 4,500 words

Original analysis or argument on a question of European law and its development.

Report

≈ 2,000 words

An evidence-led account of how a rule, ruling or policy is working in practice.

Policy Suggestion Paper

≈ 1,500 words

Concrete, actionable recommendations addressed to a defined decision-maker.

Short Opinion Article

Up to 1,000 words

A sharp, timely take on a development in any of our fields — digital, migration, environment or governance.

How submission works

Submissions are reviewed on a rolling basis. We aim to respond within three weeks. There is no fee to submit or to publish. You can use any email address — a personal Gmail account is perfectly fine, you don't need an institutional one.

  1. 1Pick your track (Students or Developed Scholar) and your format.
  2. 2Complete the form, including a short abstract of 150–200 words.
  3. 3Email your manuscript as a .docx or PDF to our submissions desk.
  4. 4An editor is assigned; you receive feedback and, if accepted, a publication date.

House style — requiredAll submissions must use the Harvard (author–date) referencing system: in-text citations in the form (Author Year: page) and a complete alphabetical reference list at the end. Manuscripts not in Harvard style will be returned for correction before they enter review.
